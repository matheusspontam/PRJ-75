\section{Propulsion --- Two-Stage Solid}

Propulsion is solid only, in two pulses (\texttt{dimensionamento\_exocet.m}, parameters in \texttt{DadosMissilComum.m}).

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Booster --- star grain.} High burn-area/volume ratio gives high, short thrust; it takes the missile from rest to $\approx$ Mach $0.93$. $M_{pB}=125~\text{kg}$, $F_B\approx 67.4~\text{kN}$, $T_{qB}=4~\text{s}$, $I_{sp}\approx 220~\text{s}$.
    \item \textbf{Sustainer --- end-burner grain.} Burns only on the face (constant area) giving low, long thrust; it holds cruise for $\approx 229~\text{s}$. $M_{pS}=330~\text{kg}$, $F_S=3.25~\text{kN}$, $T_{qS}=228.6~\text{s}$, $I_{sp}\approx 240~\text{s}$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Critical Decision --- Sizing the Sustainer}

The classic mistake is to size the sustainer for the drag at $\alpha=0$ ($C_D\approx 0.42\Rightarrow F_S\approx 2.3~\text{kN}$). But the missile cruises at a \emph{trim} angle of attack, where the real drag rises to $C_{D,\text{trim}}\approx 0.58$. Sized for $\alpha=0$, thrust $<$ drag, and the missile decelerates and dives. The sustainer is therefore sized for the cruise drag with lift (trim):

\[
    F_S=\bar q\,S\,C_{D,\text{trim}},\qquad
    \bar q=\tfrac12\rho V^2,\qquad
    V=0.9\,a,\qquad
    M_{pS}=\frac{F_S\,T_{qS}}{I_{sp}\,g}.
\]

With $C_{D,\text{trim}}\approx 0.58$ this gives $F_S=3.25~\text{kN}$: thrust equals drag at Mach $0.90$ and cruise holds.
